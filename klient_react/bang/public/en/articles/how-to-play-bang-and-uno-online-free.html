How to Play Bang! and UNO Online (And Why I Had to Code It Myself)

Written by Honza | Engine Creator

Playing board and card games is, in my opinion, the best way to spend time with friends. But a few years ago during the holidays, we ran into a problem—we were all in different places, but the urge to shoot each other in a game of Bang! wouldn't go away. I looked for a decent online alternative, but nothing really fit the bill. So, I thought to myself: "I'm a programmer, right? I'll just write it myself."

And that's how my custom multiplayer engine was born. Since then, it has grown significantly, and today it hosts several games that you can play with your friends for free, right in your browser.

Bang! – The Wild West in Your Living Room (Without Rule Disputes)

You know the drill. Someone plays a Volcanic, another claims they are covered by a Barrel, and a third argues whether you can even reach them over their Mustang... Bang! is an amazing game, but keeping track of all the rules and ranges can be a headache.

In my online version, that hassle is gone. The server accurately calculates distances, knows exactly who you can target, and smoothly guides you through your turn. All you have to do is focus on your hidden role—are you the uncompromising Sheriff, a ruthless Outlaw, or the Renegade waiting for the perfect moment? The game fully supports Drag & Drop, so it feels completely natural on both a mouse and a touch screen.

UNO and Prší Just a Click Away

Sometimes you're not in the mood for complex strategies and just want a quick five-minute game to clear your head. That's why I added the world-famous UNO and its traditional Czech ancestor, Prší (a shedding game similar to Crazy Eights). There's no installation required. Just create a room, send the unique game code on Discord or Messenger, and start dropping +2s on your friends.

Features That Won't Drive You Crazy

As a player, it infuriated me when my internet dropped halfway through a match in other games, and everything was lost. That's why I focused heavily on a few quality-of-life details:

  • Reconnect System: Accidentally closed the tab? Wi-Fi dropped? Don't panic. My engine remembers your state. When you come back, it prompts you to reconnect right where you left off.
  • Multilingual Support (i18n): Playing with an international group? The interface can easily be switched between English and Czech.
  • Dark Mode: Most of us play late at night. You only have one pair of eyes, so just toggle Dark Mode on the web and save them from the glaring white light.
